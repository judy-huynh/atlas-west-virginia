Methodology

Every column in wv_county_master.csv: what it is, where it came from, and what analysis produced it. 55 counties, 55 columns, joined on 5-digit county FIPS.

Direct value taken from the source, unchanged Derived arithmetic or a lookup Spatial geometry operation on the polygons
Before publishing any floodplain figure, confirm the source and vintage of the floodplain layer. wv_floodplain.geojson carries a single attribute, zone = SFHA, and no effective date, no NFHL panel reference, and no flood-zone breakdown. "SFHA" is FEMA's Special Flood Hazard Area, which is by definition the 1% annual chance floodplain (the "100-year floodplain"), so that is what is being measured. Which edition of the map it was cut from is not recorded anywhere in the file. The same gap applies to the buyout, repetitive-loss, and watershed layers. The National Risk Index is the only layer that self-documents its version (December 2025).

Spatial analysis performed

Four operations produced the columns that did not exist in any source. All geometry work was done in EPSG:5070 (CONUS Albers equal-area), because area cannot be measured correctly in latitude and longitude degrees. Source files are EPSG:4326.

OperationWhat it does
Point-in-polygon joinEach of the 2,826 buyout points is tested against the 55 county polygons and assigned to the county that contains it. Used because the source community field mixes county names ("McDowell County*") with town names ("Welch") across 111 values and is not a reliable key. All 2,826 points fell inside a West Virginia county, none unmatched, which confirms the join.
Polygon intersectionThe statewide floodplain polygon is clipped against each county polygon, and the area of the resulting overlap is measured. The floodplain layer is not topologically valid (self-intersections at shared edges, common in FEMA flood layers), so each part is repaired with make_valid before the layers are dissolved and clipped.
Polygon overlapEach county is overlapped against the 33 HUC-8 watersheds. The watershed with the largest overlap becomes the county's primary watershed. An overlap must cover at least 1% of the county to count. Without that threshold, all 8 Eastern Panhandle counties appear to span both basins on 0.0 to 0.2% slivers along the continental divide, which is a geometry artifact rather than a fact. With it, only Monroe County genuinely spans both.
Area measurementCounty and floodplain areas are measured in equal-area projection and converted to square miles. Cross-check: county areas sum to 24,475 sq mi against West Virginia's actual ~24,230, about 1% over, which is the expected error from polygon generalization.

Watershed and basin

33 HUC-8 watersheds. Basin comes from the HUC-8 prefix: 02 is Mid-Atlantic (Chesapeake Bay), 05 is Ohio River. West Virginia splits 47 Ohio River counties to 8 Chesapeake Bay counties. Source: USGS Watershed Boundary Dataset (vintage not recorded in the file).

Reproducing all of it. Every calculated column is produced by one script, tools/build_wv_master.py. Run pip install shapely pyproj then python3 tools/build_wv_master.py. Nothing was done by hand, in Excel, or in QGIS, so there are no manual steps to trust.
Open questions before publication. The floodplain, buyout, repetitive-loss, and watershed layers carry no source or date metadata. The floodplain vintage in particular needs to be confirmed before any floodplain percentage is published. The National Risk Index is the only layer that declares its own version (December 2025).
Two FEMA totals, both correct, easily confused. West Virginia's total FEMA PA+HM is $694,455,589. The county-allocated portion, which is what FEMA_PA_HM sums to, is $225,705,731. They are different measures and should not be used interchangeably.
